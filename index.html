Athens Memory

I had been thinking of visiting this city since I was a young child. Once there, I had the best possible visit. I met someone who showed me the insider's view of Athens. An unforgettable experience.

Memories of Athens

Viki was an amazing host in Athens. She showed me lots of local, in-the-know spots, including a great local beach that few know about a short drive away from the city. -- What can one say about a man who writes poetry at night and makes sandals during the day? A soul maker! I still wear mine. -- I want to make an emoji out of that mosaic.

One evening I was taken to a great party where I was introduced to the cultural touchstone known as Eurovision. Ktich at its best. The party lasted all night and the hosts made breakfast for everybody in the dawn hours. Hospitality at its finest. (Notwist was the part of the soundtrack. Pilot is off that album.)

Rhodes

Refreshing Rhodes

I stayed in the Old Town of Rhodes, a medieval walled city, which was like a step back in time. Here is a photo of the breakfast spot where each morning I ate classically tasty homemade Greek yogurt. Although the weather was very hot, my room was comfortable and cool as the walls were made of incredibly large stones. The photo is of the Lindos Acropolis at the western tip of the island surrounded by incredible beaches.

In a small family shop who make everything themselves, I found the nicest summer sweater the color of the Mediterrean which I still cherish. ( I listened to Unplugged by Nirvana, especially Kurt doing a Bowie cover.)

Patmos

Peaceful Patmos

Patmos is for peace and serenity. It has an endless number of beautiful and deserted beaches, quaint settlements, and an enlightened art scene. There is a religious history to island too as it was where Saint John lived in a cave, and the Byzantines built a monastery there over 1000 years ago. Idyllic easily comes to mind as an apt description. While there, I ate mellow food and listened to mellow, surfy music. (Come and Hug Me by Laurent Michel Lombard)

I could go to several different beaches a day and not see a soul in sihgt. Locals only go to the beaches on special occasions or holidays.
